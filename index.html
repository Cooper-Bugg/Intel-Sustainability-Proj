Sustainability Through the Ages

Explore Intel's journey through time, discovering how our commitment to innovation has shaped a more sustainable future for technology and our planet.

1968

Intel Founded

Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore rename NM Electronics to Intel Corporation, laying the foundation for decades of technological innovation. Intel begins its journey toward innovation and sustainability.

1971

First Microprocessor

Intel debuts the 4004, the world's first commercial microprocessor, igniting the microprocessor revolution and propelling the future of computing devices. The 4004 sets the stage for energy-efficient computing.

1978

8086 Processor

Launch of the 8086 processor, establishing the x86 architecture that drives countless PCs and servers in the modern era. x86 architecture enables scalable, sustainable computing.

1985

386 Processor

Intel introduces the 386 processor with 32-bit architecture, ushering in a new era of performance and multitasking for personal computers. 32-bit architecture improves efficiency and sustainability.

2006

Peak GHG Emissions

This year marks Intel's highest annual greenhouse gas emissions for operations. Over subsequent years, Intel invests heavily in chemical abatement, renewable energy, and energy-efficient manufacturing to reverse this trend. Intel begins aggressive sustainability initiatives.

2020

RISE Strategy

Intel launches its RISE (Responsible, Inclusive, Sustainable, Enabling) strategy and 2030 goals, aiming to drive industry-wide progress on climate action, water stewardship, and waste reduction. RISE strategy sets ambitious sustainability goals.

2022

Net-Zero By 2040

Intel announces its commitment to achieve net-zero greenhouse gas emissions (Scope 1 and 2) across its global operations by 2040, building on years of environmental initiatives. Net-zero pledge accelerates environmental progress.

2023

Renewable Electricity

The company achieves 99% renewable electricity usage worldwide, helping to drastically lower carbon emissions and driving progress toward Intel's long-term sustainability goals. 99% renewable electricity milestone reached.

2024

Sustainability Summit

Intel hosts its first Sustainability Summit, uniting suppliers, government officials, and industry leaders to collaborate on next-generation sustainable semiconductor manufacturing. Summit drives collaboration for a sustainable future.
